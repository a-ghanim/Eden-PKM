Eden: PKM System Spec
The Tab Overflow Killer
Core Problem
People accumulate 50+ tabs, 1000+ bookmarks, scattered notes across apps. Nothing surfaces the right information at the right time. Matter died. Raindrop is just organized hoarding. We need something that actually thinks.
The Big Idea
Eden is your second brain that learns what matters to you. One-click save from anywhere â†’ AI automatically categorizes, extracts insights, surfaces connections, and reminds you of relevant content when you need it.

Feature Set (32-hour MVP)
1. Universal Capture

Browser extension: One-click save (tab, selection, or full page)
Auto-save: Option to automatically capture all tabs at end of day
Multi-format support: Web pages, PDFs, tweets, YouTube videos (transcript extraction)
Mobile share sheet: Save from phone apps

2. Intelligent Organization (The Magic)

Auto-tagging: AI categorizes content by topic, intent (read later, reference, inspiration, tutorial)
Smart collections: Automatically groups related content ("React performance optimization", "Startup marketing tactics")
Temporal awareness: Separates ephemeral content (news, deals) from evergreen (how-tos, research)
Duplicate detection: "You saved this 3 times already" with merge option

3. Knowledge Graph

Concept extraction: Pull out key ideas, people, companies, technologies mentioned
Connection mapping: "This article relates to 3 other things you saved about YC application advice"
Visual graph view: See how your saved content clusters and connects

4. Proactive Resurfacing (The Killer Feature)

Context-aware suggestions: Writing an email about pricing? Eden surfaces that SaaS pricing article you saved 2 months ago
Smart digests: Weekly "Here's what you saved but never read, organized by priority"
Scheduled reminders: "Review your saved fundraising materials in 3 months when you're ready"
Chat interface: "What have I saved about Next.js server components?"

5. Powerful Search

Natural language: "That thread about founder burnout from someone at Stripe"
Semantic search: Finds conceptually similar content, not just keyword matches
Filter by: date, domain, tag, collection, read/unread status
Saved searches: Create persistent queries like "Unread marketing articles from last month"

6. Tab Management Integration

Tab graveyard: Save all tabs, close them, resurrect later with context
Session snapshots: Capture entire browsing sessions ("Research for SF trip", "Fundraise prep")
Smart restore: "Open all my ML research tabs" across multiple windows

7. Minimalist Reading Experience

Clean reader mode: Distraction-free article view
Highlights & notes: Annotate with keyboard shortcuts
TTS integration: Listen to articles
Progress tracking: Pick up where you left off


UX Principles
Speed above all: Capture must be <2 clicks. Search must feel instant.
Invisible intelligence: AI works behind the scenes. Users shouldn't think about tagging or organizing.
Trust through transparency: Show WHY Eden suggests connections or collections.
Beautiful constraints: Limited color palette, generous whitespace, focus on typography.

Frontend

React (Replit deployment)
Tailwind for styling

Backend

Content summarization
